Kateel Durga Parameshwari Temple

History of the Famous Temple in Dakshina Kannada

Introduction

Kateel Durga Parameshwari Temple is one of the most famous temples in Dakshina Kannada district of Karnataka. The temple is located on an island in the middle of the Nandini River in a small town called Kateel, about 29 km from Mangalore.

History of the Temple

According to legend, the temple is dedicated to Goddess Durga Parameshwari who appeared to protect the region from a demon named Arunasura. It is believed that the goddess destroyed the demon and saved the people of the area.

The temple has a long history and has been an important religious center for centuries. Devotees from different parts of Karnataka and India visit the temple to seek blessings from the goddess.

Special Features

  • The temple is built in traditional South Indian style.
  • It is located in the middle of the Nandini River.
  • Thousands of devotees visit during festivals.
  • The temple is famous for Yakshagana performances.

Festivals

The temple celebrates many festivals throughout the year. The most important festival is Navaratri, when the temple is decorated and special prayers are performed for Goddess Durga.
